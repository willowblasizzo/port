About Muddy Water Photography

There's only one way to get to Sandy Lake and that is by plane. Whether you're returning home or coming for a visit, it's clear you've arrived when the lakes suddenly turn from a deep blue to a murky brown (see what I did there?).

There is much that makes Sandy Lake First Nation a special and unique place. It is the vision of Muddy Water Photography to explore, celebrate and preserve the richness of its people and all that encompasses the beautiful lifestyle and culture that has inhabited the lands along the shores of the muddy waters and beyond for hundreds of years.

Noochench Little Grandchild

In Oji-Cree there are different variations of spelling, depending on how one chooses to pronounce the word and in what context. Noosis is the Oji-Cree word for grandchild. Noochichench translates into little grandchild. Often, spelling and pronunciation become two different things. Indigenous languages aren't traditionally formulated into dictionaries and there is beauty in how adaptable the language is to different families, communities and regions.

Noochench is the affectionate term used by gokums (grandmothers) and gomshooms (grandfathers) for their noosis (grandchild). Here are some of my favorite portraits of my noochench Jacob with his parents.
